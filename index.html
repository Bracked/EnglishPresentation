Vint Cerf Biography

Cerf was born June 23, 1943 in New Haven, Connecticut

After college, Cerf worked at IBM as engenier for two years.

His personal interests include fine wine, gourmet cooking and science fiction. Cerf and his wife, Sigrid, were married in 1966 and have two sons

Cerf has worked for Google as a vice president and Chief Internet Evangelist since October 2005. In this function he has become well known for his predictions on how technology will affect future society, encompassing such areas as artificial intelligence, environmentalism, the advent of IPv6 and the transformation of the television industry and its delivery model.

Bob Kahn Biography

Kahn was born December 23, 1938 in New York

After receiving a B.E.E. degree in electrical engineering in 1960, Kahn went on to Princeton University where he earned a Master of Art in 1962 and Ph.D. in 1964, both in electrical engineering.

He first worked at Bolt Beranek and Newman Inc., then in 1972 joined the Information Processing Techniques Office (IPTO) within DARPA.

In the fall of 1972, he demonstrated the ARPANET by connecting 20 different computers at the International Computer Communication Conference

After he became director of IPTO, he started the United States government's billion dollar Strategic Computing Initiative, the largest computer research and development program ever undertaken by the U.S. federal government

Tim Berners-Lee

Tim Berners-Lee is the inventor of the World Wide Web and he created a new computer language called HTML (Hypertext Markup Language) which most web pages are written in. The first web page was available on 6 August 1991.
